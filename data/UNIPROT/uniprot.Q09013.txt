CC   -!- DISEASE: Defects in DMPK are the cause of dystrophia myotonica
CC       type 1 (DM1) [MIM:160900]; also known as Steinert disease. A
CC       muscular disorder characterized by myotonia, muscle wasting in the
CC       distal extremities, cataract, hypogonadism, defective endocrine
CC       functions, male baldness and cardiac arrhythmias. Note=The
CC       causative mutation is a CTG expansion in the 3'-UTR of the DMPK
CC       gene. A length exceeding 50 CTG repeats is pathogenic, while
CC       normal individuals have 5 to 37 repeats. Intermediate alleles with
CC       35-49 triplets are not disease-causing but show instability in
CC       intergenerational transmissions. Disease severity varies with the
CC       number of repeats: mildly affected persons have 50 to 150 repeats,
CC       patients with classic DM have 100 to 1,000 repeats, and those with
CC       congenital onset can have more than 2,000 repeats.
