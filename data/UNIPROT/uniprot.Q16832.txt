CC   -!- FUNCTION: This tyrosine kinase receptor for fibrillar collagen
CC       mediates fibroblast migration and proliferation. Contributes to
CC       cutaneous wound healing (By similarity).
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + a [protein]-L-tyrosine = ADP + a
CC       [protein]-L-tyrosine phosphate.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Membrane; Single-pass type I membrane
CC       protein.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: The major 10 kDa transcript is expressed in
CC       high levels in heart and lung, less in brain, placenta, liver,
CC       skeletal muscle, pancreas, and kidney.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Defects in DDR2 are the cause of spondyloepimetaphyseal
CC       dysplasia short limb-hand type (SEMD-SL) [MIM:271665]. A bone
CC       disease characterized by short-limbed dwarfism, a narrow chest
CC       with pectus excavatum, brachydactyly in the hands and feet, a
CC       characteristic craniofacial appearance and premature
CC       calcifications. The radiological findings are distinctive and
CC       comprise short long bones throughout the skeleton with striking
CC       epiphyses that are stippled, flattened and fragmented and flared,
CC       irregular metaphyses. Platyspondyly in the spine with wide
CC       intervertebral spaces is observed and some vertebral bodies are
CC       pear-shaped with central humps, anterior protrusions and posterior
CC       scalloping.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the protein kinase superfamily. Tyr protein
CC       kinase family. Insulin receptor subfamily.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Contains 1 F5/8 type C domain.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Contains 1 protein kinase domain.
