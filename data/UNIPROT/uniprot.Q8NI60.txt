CC   -!- DISEASE: Defects in CABC1 are a cause of coenzyme Q10 deficiency
CC       (CoQ10 deficiency) [MIM:607426]. CoQ10 deficiency is an autosomal
CC       recessive disorder with variable manifestations. It can be
CC       associated with three main clinical phenotypes: a predominantly
CC       myopathic form with central nervous system involvement, an
CC       infantile encephalomyopathy with renal dysfunction and an ataxic
CC       form with cerebellar atrophy.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Defects in CABC1 are the cause of spinocerebellar ataxia
CC       autosomal recessive type 9 (SCAR9) [MIM:612016]; also known as
CC       autosomal recessive cerebellar ataxia type 2 (ARCA2).
CC       Spinocerebellar ataxia is a clinically and genetically
CC       heterogeneous group of cerebellar disorders. Patients show
CC       progressive incoordination of gait and often poor coordination of
CC       hands, speech and eye movements, due to degeneration of the
CC       cerebellum with variable involvement of the brainstem and spinal
CC       cord. SCAR9 is an autosomal recessive form characterized by gait
CC       ataxia and cerebellar atrophy with slow progression and few
CC       associated features. Patients can manifest brisk tendon reflexes
CC       and Hoffmann sign, mild psychomotor retardation, mild axonal
CC       degeneration of the sural nerve, exercise intolerance and elevated
CC       serum lactate.
