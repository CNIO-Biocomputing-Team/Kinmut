CC   -!- FUNCTION: Disrupts insulin signaling by binding directly to Akt
CC       kinases and blocking their activation. May bind directly to and
CC       mask the 'Thr-308' phosphorylation site in AKT1. Binds to ATF4 and
CC       inhibits its transcriptional activation activity. Interacts with
CC       the NF-kappa-B transactivator p65 RELA and inhibits its
CC       phosphorylation and thus its transcriptional activation activity.
CC       Interacts with MAPK kinases and regulates activation of MAP
CC       kinases. May play a role in programmed neuronal cell death but
CC       does not appear to affect non-neuronal cells. Does not display
CC       kinase activity.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with AKT1, AKT2, ATF4, MAP2K1 and MAP2K7.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       P18848:ATF4; NbExp=1; IntAct=EBI-492476, EBI-492498;
CC       Q02750:MAP2K1; NbExp=1; IntAct=EBI-492476, EBI-492564;
CC       O14733:MAP2K7; NbExp=1; IntAct=EBI-492476, EBI-492605;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus (Probable).
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highest expression in liver, pancreas,
CC       peripheral blood leukocytes and bone marrow. Also highly expressed
CC       in a number of primary lung, colon and breast tumors. Expressed in
CC       spleen, thymus, and prostate and is undetectable in other examined
CC       tissues, including testis, ovary, small intestine, colon,
CC       leukocyte, heart, brain, placenta, lung, skeletal muscle, and
CC       kidney.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: By hypoxia, TNF and by nutrient starvation. Expression
CC       is PI 3-kinase and/or NF-kappa-B-dependent.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the protein kinase superfamily. CAMK
CC       Ser/Thr protein kinase family. Tribbles subfamily.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Contains 1 protein kinase domain.
CC   -!- CAUTION: The role of this protein in Akt activation has been
CC       demonstrated by Du et al (PubMed:12791994) for the mouse ortholog
CC       but Iynedjian (PubMed:15469416) has not been able to reproduce the
CC       result in rat hepatocytes.
