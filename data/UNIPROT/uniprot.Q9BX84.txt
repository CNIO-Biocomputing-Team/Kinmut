CC   -!- FUNCTION: Essential ion channel and serine/threonine-protein
CC       kinase. Crucial for magnesium homeostasis. Has an important role
CC       in epithelial magnesium transport and in the active magnesium
CC       absorption in the gut and kidney. Isoforms of the type M6-kinase
CC       lack the ion channel region.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + a protein = ADP + a phosphoprotein.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms heterodimers with TRPM7. TRPM6 requires the
CC       presence of TRPM7 to be targeted to the cell membrane (in HEK 293
CC       cells).
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Membrane; Multi-pass membrane protein.

CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highly expressed in kidney and colon. Isoform
CC       TRPM6a and isoform TRPM6b, are coexpressed with TRPM7 in kidney,
CC       and testis, and are also found in several cell lines of lung
CC       origin. Isoform TRPM6c is detected only in testis and in H510
CC       small cell lung carcinoma cells.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Defects in TRPM6 are the cause of hypomagnesemia type 1
CC       (HOMG1) [MIM:602014]; also known as hypomagnesemia with secondary
CC       hypocalcemia (HSH). HOMG1 is a disorder due to a primary defect in
CC       intestinal magnesium absorption. It is characterized by low levels
CC       of serum magnesium alongside with a normal renal magnesium
CC       secretion, secondary hypocalcemia and calcinocis. Affected
CC       individuals show neurologic symptoms of hypomagnesemic
CC       hypocalcemia, including seizures and muscle spasms, during
CC       infancy. Hypocalcemia is secondary to parathyroid failure
CC       resulting from magnesium deficiency. Untreated, the disorder may
CC       be fatal or may result in neurological damage.

CC       subfamily.
